Sunlight Zone

Epipelagic

Where sunlight pierces water and life thrives in abundance. This vibrant layer hosts 90% of all marine life — coral reefs bloom, dolphins breach, and bioluminescent plankton drift like living stars at night.

Twilight Zone

Mesopelagic

Light fades to a cold, ghostly blue. Here, creatures have evolved extraordinary tools — bioluminescence, enormous eyes, and the ability to withstand pressures 100x greater than at the surface.

Midnight Zone

Bathypelagic

Total darkness. No sunlight ever reaches here. Cold (2–4°C), crushing pressure, and complete blackness — yet life persists in astonishing, alien forms. This is the largest living space on Earth, mostly unexplored.

The Hadal Zone

Challenger Deep · Mariana Trench

The deepest place on Earth. At 10,935 meters below sea level, the pressure is 1,100 atmospheres — enough to implode a submarine instantly. Yet here, in the absolute dark, tiny amphipods and snailfish thrive where nothing should exist.
